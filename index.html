The Green Carbon Valley

Vision: To turn organic waste into something useful (biochar) that helps farmers and protects the planet.

The Challenge & Our Purpose

The Problem

  • The effects of climate change, such as increased temperatures and changed rainfall patterns, are negatively contributing to Kenya's agriculture.
  • This instability leads to food insecurity and economic hardship for farming communities.
  • Severe soil degradation, including declining fertility and water holding issues, is worsening.
  • There is a lack of localized ways to characterize biochar for different soils, reducing its effectiveness.

Our Purpose

  • We propose utilizing biochar to actively reduce carbon emissions.
  • Our solution is environmentally friendly, supporting the reduction of waste by converting organic waste into biochar.
  • Biochar significantly improves soil health by increasing carbon content and the soil's ability to retain water for longer periods.

Our Technology: The Double Drum Retort

Engineered for sustainable, decentralized biochar production.

Our system utilizes a modular, skid-mounted batch pyrolysis system designed with a double drum retort configuration.

  • 1. Separated Zones: The design separates the combustion and pyrolysis zones. The outer chamber is the combustion furnace, while the inner sealed drum acts as an oxygen-limited pyrolysis chamber.
  • 2. Indirect Heating: Biomass is heated indirectly at operating temperatures of 450-550°C.
  • 3. Efficiency: Volatile gases released during the process are redirected to the furnace for combustion, which significantly reduces the need for external fuel.
  • 4. Yield: Operating on 2-4 hour batch times, the system yields 25-35% biochar by mass.
